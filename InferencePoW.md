## 1. Scope and Stakes

Topic D asks whether a proof-of-work puzzle should stop fighting custom silicon and instead anchor itself to the largest, most liquid hardware fleet on earth: GPUs and accelerators built for neural-network inference. The argument for inversion is economic — commodity-anchoring was always the real goal of ASIC resistance, and tensor-capable GPUs are the modern commodity — plus an option on usefulness: a puzzle whose inner loop is low-precision matrix multiplication is at least the same *shape* as paid AI work, even before it is actually useful. The stakes are the credibility of the whole design class: if consensus-grade verification of tensor work is impossible or the economics collapse into ordinary mining, the inversion is a rebranding, not an architecture.

The framing question changed between the writing of the [FableFrontiers.md](FableFrontiers.md) plan and this document. Section 9.4 of the plan called prior art "thin, which is the opportunity." It is no longer thin: a proof-of-useful-work chain built on exactly this thesis — Pearl, running noisy matrix multiplication on consumer tensor cores — launched mainnet on April 27, 2026 and promptly attracted both a GPU mining rush and a peer-reviewed critique of its usefulness claims [Reported: [Hashrate Index analysis](https://hashrateindex.com/blog/pearl-prl-ai-compute-cryptocurrency/), [usefulness-gap study](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2606.04819)]. The document therefore does double duty: survey of a design class that just went live, and post-mortem of the plan's central empirical bet.

## 2. State of the Art

The lineage runs from gesture to architecture in under a decade: early designs put matrix arithmetic in the hash for ASIC-shaping reasons, the middle generation solved verification of ML work in non-consensus settings, and the current generation closes the loop with succinct proofs over the matrix multiplication itself.

| System | Year | Core mechanism | Arithmetic | Verification | Status |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| [Tensority](https://github.com/Bytom/bytom), Bytom | 2018 | Tensor ops inside the block hash | Integer matrix | Recompute | Defunct in practice; premature by one hardware generation |
| [Optical PoW](https://arxiv.org/abs/1911.05193), later kHeavyHash on [Kaspa](https://github.com/kaspanet/rusty-kaspa) | 2019–21 | Small matrix multiply inside a hash sandwich | Low-bit integer | Recompute, matrix is tiny | Live; shaped for photonics, mined on ASICs — an ASIC-resistance cautionary tale |
| [TOPLOC](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2501.16007), Prime Intellect | 2025 | Locality-sensitive hash of activations for verifiable inference | FP16 activations, tolerance-based | Top-k activation commitments, ~100x cheaper than re-inference, 258 bytes per 32 tokens | Production in [INTELLECT-2](https://www.primeintellect.ai/blog/intellect-2); service verification, not consensus |
| cuPOW, "[Proofs of Useful Work from Arbitrary Matrix Multiplication](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2504.09971)," Komargodski–Weinstein et al. | Apr 2025 | NoisyGEMM: noise-perturbed GEMM hashed into a commitment with a proof wrapper, claimed 1+o(1) overhead versus plain GEMM | GPU tensor-core GEMM | Succinct proof, cheap on-chain check | Theory basis for Pearl [Reported] |
| [Pearl](https://github.com/pearl-research-labs/pearl), PRL mainnet | Apr 2026 | cuPOW as L1 consensus, ~2-minute blocks, 2.1B supply cap | Tensor-core GEMM on RTX 2000+ | Succinct proof per block | Live; ~$146M market cap and roughly 49% mining-revenue decay within weeks of launch [Reported: [Tom's Hardware](https://www.tomshardware.com/tech-industry/cryptomining/new-ai-compute-cryptocurrency-pearl-sparks-a-gpu-mining-rush-but-profitability-is-sliding)] |
| [TensorZKP](https://eprint.iacr.org/2026/1250) | 2026 | Tensor cores as the *prover* for general ZK proofs via tensor-core-compatible finite-field arithmetic | Finite field mapped onto low-precision MMA | 2^25-gate proof in 215 ms; 955x over CPU, 36.2x over SIMT GPU baselines | Research framework [Reported] |

Two adjacent results define the determinism boundary the design class must respect. At the instruction level, vendor tensor units are not IEEE-754 compliant and differ across vendors and even GPU generations in floating-point accumulation behavior [Reported: [Accurate Models of NVIDIA Tensor Cores](https://arxiv.org/abs/2512.07004)]; at the kernel level, accumulation order varies with batch size and kernel selection, producing the batch-variance that reproducibility systems like [HEAL](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2606.21023) and [Hawkeye](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2603.20421) exist to fight [Reported: [floating-point noise structure study](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2511.00025)]. Integer accumulation has no such problem — integer addition is associative, so an INT8-input, INT32-accumulate matrix multiply is bit-exact regardless of reduction order or vendor [Reported at instruction level in the tensor-core modeling literature; the consensus-grade cross-vendor claim is Modeled from associativity and is the residual prototype question].

## 3. Analysis

The canonical construction, common to cuPOW and to any sound design in this class, has five stages; the design freedom is almost entirely in stage four.

```mermaid
graph LR
    A["Seed from block header and nonce"] --> B["PRG-expand to matrices"]
    B --> C["Low-precision GEMM on tensor units"]
    C --> D["Commitment plus succinct proof"]
    D --> E["Difficulty check on commitment"]
```

**Verification economics.** The naive verifier recomputes the GEMM: for n×n matrices that is 2n³ multiply-accumulates, 1.37e11 for n = 4,096 — a non-starter for every full node. [Freivalds' check](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Freivalds%27_algorithm) — test A(Bx) = Cx for a random vector x — cuts this to about 3n² multiply-accumulates, a ratio of roughly 2n/3, about 2,700x at n = 4,096 [Modeled]. But Freivalds is not free at the system level: the verifier needs the operand matrices, which are n²-sized, so either the chain regenerates them from the seed (O(n²) PRG work, comparable to the check itself) or the miner ships megabytes per block; soundness amplification multiplies either cost. This is why the field converged on succinct proofs instead: the cuPOW claim of 1+o(1) prover overhead with a cheap on-chain verifier, and TensorZKP's demonstration that the *proving itself* can run on tensor cores, together close the verifier's dilemma for matrix work in a way Freivalds-style spot-checking cannot [Reported for the claims; the cuPOW overhead figure is the single most load-bearing unreproduced number in this document].

**Determinism.** Consensus requires that every node reach the same validity bit, which forbids the tolerance thresholds that make TOPLOC work — TOPLOC's robust hashing absorbs GPU non-determinism precisely because verification-of-service can accept "close enough," and consensus cannot. The instruction-level literature above then forces the conclusion: the puzzle's arithmetic must be integer or finite-field, where associativity makes results order-independent and therefore vendor-independent, and floating-point tensor paths are disqualified no matter how fast — a conclusion Section 4.3 refines to "disqualified for any hardware-diverse chain" in light of the batch-invariant kernel results [Modeled from Reported instruction-level results]. TensorZKP's finite-field-on-tensor-core arithmetic is the demonstration that this constraint costs little performance. The FableFrontiers.md Section 9.4 risk line — that determinism was the empirical unknown requiring first measurement — is thus half-resolved from the literature: the floating-point path is confirmed dead, the integer path is near-certain but still wants a cross-vendor bit-exactness test as cheap insurance.

**Progress-freeness and amortization.** Nonce-seeded matrix generation makes attempts independent, preserving progress-freeness; the tension is that PRG-expanding fresh operands per attempt wastes the bandwidth that batched GEMM exists to exploit. Pearl's NoisyGEMM formulation — extracting proofs from matrix products that occur *anyway* inside inference and training passes — is the elegant resolution in principle, since the workload provides operand variety for free. Whether its noise injection preserves amortization resistance when a miner controls workload selection is not publicly analyzable from available sources [Speculative; flagged as the design-soundness question an audit should answer first].

**Economics.** Pearl's launch supplies the first real data on the inversion thesis, and it reads as classic PoW economics wearing new hardware: an RTX 5090 earned about $17.19/day in early June 2026, roughly half its April figure, as difficulty tracked the mining rush [Reported: Tom's Hardware]. Difficulty adjustment guarantees that steady-state mining revenue converges toward marginal electricity cost regardless of how AI-shaped the work is; the durable economic claim is therefore not miner profit but *fleet liquidity* — idle inference capacity gets a permissionless revenue floor and a zero-switching-cost alternative to spot rental markets, which disciplines rental pricing [Modeled]. The usefulness side is where the launch data is unkind: the empirical study of the live network found the work is overwhelmingly speculative — inference nobody requested or pays for — with a single paid-demand endpoint (the [Together AI partnership](https://www.together.ai/blog/together-ai-partners-with-pearl-research-labs)) against a fleet of speculative miners; "AI-shaped proof-of-work" rather than proof of useful work [Reported: [arXiv 2606.04819](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2606.04819), Hashrate Index]. This cleanly vindicates the plan's separation of Topic D (hardware anchoring) from Topic C (usefulness): Pearl achieved the first without the second, and the two claims should never again be evaluated as one.

**Centralization surface.** Inverting ASIC resistance does not escape the ASIC question; it relocates it. The "ASIC" for an INT8-GEMM puzzle already exists as the TPU and its relatives, so the decentralization exposure shifts from covert ASIC development to overt hyperscaler fleet dominance — a different threat model with different politics: the hardware is general-purpose and its owners have reputational and opportunity-cost reasons not to mine, but nothing structural prevents them [Speculative, flagged for the synthesis]. Parameter agility cuts the same way as in classic PoW: a puzzle tuned to today's microscaling formats ages with the hardware only if governance can retune it, and every retuning is a fork fight.

## 4. Voices and Dissent

The institutional record on this topic — protocol papers, launch announcements, partnership posts — is barely a year old and almost entirely written by parties with positions; the corrective voices are researchers who measured the live network, a theorist who formalized why the economics cannot work as marketed, and kernel engineers whose determinism results complicate this document's own analysis. They are recorded here with their specific claims because on a topic this young, the critique *is* the literature. Three voices follow: the empiricists, the theorist, the kernel engineers.

### 4.1 Empiricists

The empiricists measured the live network and found the usefulness claim empty in practice. The [usefulness-gap study](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2606.04819) of Pearl's deployed protocol is specific in a way no institutional source is: the dominant mining software contains no AI inference code at all — every analyzed worker runs a pure GEMM miner on inference-capable hardware; the protocol's verification confirms that a multiplication was performed correctly but never checks whether the operand matrices came from a model, a customer, or anything but a random generator; and the obvious fix, statistically distinguishing model-derived from random matrices, is defeated by adversarial Gaussian sampling. The scale claims are stark: on the order of 320,000 RTX-3090-class GPUs drawing roughly 112 megawatts on what the authors characterize as zero useful AI computation, while lifting GPU rental prices around 38% [Reported: [Tom's Hardware coverage](https://www.tomshardware.com/tech-industry/artificial-intelligence/ai-cryptomining-networks-320-000-rtx-3090-class-gpus-allegedly-burn-112-megawatts-of-power-on-zero-useful-ai-computation-pearls-gpus-are-doing-random-matrix-math-study-claims); the figures are the study's and predate any network response]. The same critics read the [Together AI partnership](https://www.together.ai/blog/together-ai-partners-with-pearl-research-labs) — this document's Section 3 "one genuine demand endpoint" — less charitably: Together's endpoint inference runs on Together's own GPUs, separate from the mining network, with PRL revenue used to subsidize the endpoint's price; financial arbitrage wearing a useful-work costume rather than useful mining [Reported].

### 4.2 The Theorist

The theorist says the gap is structural, not an implementation bug. Rafael Pass's "[The Economics of Proof-of-Useful-Work](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2606.06700)" formalizes what the Pearl data shows: block-reward-subsidized useful work couples work supply to token price rather than to demand for the work; difficulty adjustment — which exists to stabilize block times — actively misaligns work performed with utility demanded; and the paper derives impossibility-style results that a PoUW system cannot simultaneously hold a stable token value, a reliable useful-work supply, and market-competitive worker compensation. Under this model, Pearl's trajectory — speculative hashrate flood, revenue collapse to electricity cost, one subsidized demand endpoint — is not early-network noise but the equilibrium [Reported; the model's assumptions merit independent scrutiny before the impossibility framing is treated as final].

### 4.3 Kernel Engineers

The kernel engineers complicate this document's determinism argument from the other direction. Horace He and colleagues at Thinking Machines showed in "[Defeating Nondeterminism in LLM Inference](https://www.nextbigfuture.com/2025/11/defeating-nondeterminism-in-llm-inference-by-thinking-machines.html)" that floating-point nondeterminism in production inference is not hardware fate but a batch-invariance property of kernel selection — and that pinning batch-invariant implementations of matmul, RMSNorm, and attention yields 1,000 consecutive bitwise-identical runs under dynamic batching, with the [batch-invariant-ops library](https://www.lmsys.org/blog/2025-09-22-sglang-deterministic/) released and reproduced in SGLang [Reported]. This softens Section 3's flat disqualification of floating point: a consensus protocol *could* pin a canonical floating-point kernel and achieve determinism per implementation. What it cannot do is achieve determinism across vendors whose matrix units disagree at the instruction level, so a pinned-kernel design trades the determinism problem for a hardware-monoculture requirement — which, for a decentralization mechanism, is arguably the same problem relocated. The integer-arithmetic conclusion survives, but as the *portable* option rather than the only option [Reported plus Modeled].

## 5. Findings

Findings are numbered for citation, most consequential first.

**F-D1.** The design class is no longer hypothetical: a tensor-core proof-of-useful-work L1 (Pearl/cuPOW) reached mainnet in April 2026 with a working succinct-verification pipeline and material hashrate. The plan's premise that nothing deployed targets inference silicon is obsolete; the open space is now soundness analysis, competing constructions, and second-mover corrections [Reported].

**F-D2.** Hardware anchoring and usefulness are empirically separable, and Pearl demonstrates the first without the second: difficulty-driven revenue decay identical to classic PoW, work product overwhelmingly unpaid and unrequested, one genuine demand endpoint. Topic D's thesis stands or falls on anchoring economics alone; usefulness remains Topic C's unsolved problem [Reported: arXiv 2606.04819].

**F-D3.** Consensus-grade verification of matrix work is solved in principle by succinct proofs, not by spot-checking: Freivalds-class protocols carry O(n²) verifier data costs that succinct commitments avoid, and the cuPOW 1+o(1) prover-overhead claim — if it reproduces — removes the last order-of-magnitude objection. That claim is unreproduced outside its authors and is the highest-value measurement target in this topic [Reported claim, Modeled surrounding analysis].

**F-D4.** The puzzle arithmetic must be integer or finite-field. Floating-point tensor paths are non-reproducible across vendors and even kernel invocations at the levels documented in the 2025–26 literature, while integer accumulation is order-independent by associativity; TensorZKP shows finite-field arithmetic maps onto tensor cores at high efficiency. Tolerance-based schemes (TOPLOC) are sound for verification-of-service and structurally unsound for consensus [Reported plus Modeled].

**F-D5.** The steady-state economics of inference-anchored PoW are ordinary PoW economics — revenue converges to marginal electricity — and the defensible value proposition is fleet liquidity and a floor under idle accelerator time, not mining profit. Business cases built on sustained miner margins should be discounted accordingly [Modeled from Reported launch data].

**F-D6.** ASIC inversion relocates rather than removes the centralization question: the natural dominant miner of a tensor puzzle is a hyperscaler fleet, an overt rather than covert threat. No deployed design addresses this; it is the class's principal unexamined risk [Speculative].

**F-D7.** The plan's Stage 4 prototype should pivot. The original microbenchmark question — is tensor-unit determinism achievable — is substantially answered from literature (F-D4); the successor questions with highest information value are reproducing the cuPOW overhead claim on independent hardware and a cross-vendor INT8 GEMM bit-exactness sweep as cheap residual verification [Modeled; supersedes the corresponding line of FableFrontiers.md Section 9.4 per the plan's supersession rule].

**F-D8.** Combined with F-B7 — PoW needs no post-quantum migration — an integer-tensor PoW chain is simultaneously quantum-robust at the consensus layer and anchored to the dominant hardware cycle, a configuration no other consensus family matches. This is the strongest cross-topic argument the program has yet produced and belongs at the center of the synthesis [Modeled from Reported components].

**F-D9.** Provenance, not verification, is the unsolved core of usefulness: cuPOW-class proofs establish that a multiplication happened, never that anyone wanted it, and distributional checks on operands are defeated by adversarial sampling. Any credible useful-work design must bind the puzzle to demand at the protocol layer — customer-signed work commitments or equivalent — rather than auditing the work's statistics after the fact [Reported: usefulness-gap study].

**F-D10.** The usefulness failure has a formal economic backbone: Pass's model shows block-reward subsidy couples work supply to token price instead of work demand, and derives that stable token value, reliable useful-work supply, and market-competitive compensation cannot hold simultaneously. This upgrades F-D2 from an empirical observation about one network to a structural claim about the class, and shifts the burden of proof onto any future PoUW design to show which leg of the trilemma it sacrifices [Reported, model-dependent].

**F-D11.** Floating-point determinism is achievable per pinned implementation — the batch-invariant kernel results are bit-exact under production batching — but not across vendors, so a floating-point consensus puzzle implies a hardware monoculture. F-D4's integer conclusion stands as the portable path; the pinned-kernel alternative exists and should be understood as trading arithmetic freedom for decentralization, the inverse of the trade this design class was created to make [Reported plus Modeled].

## 6. Conclusions

**Level 1, macro.** The inversion thesis is validated in its weak form and unproven in its strong form. Weak form — a PoW puzzle can anchor to inference silicon with consensus-grade succinct verification — is now demonstrated in production. Strong form — that such a puzzle performs work anyone pays for — failed its first market test, and the failure now has both an empirical mechanism (F-D9: proofs verify multiplication, not demand, and provenance checks are adversarially defeatable) and a theoretical one (F-D10: the Pass trilemma makes subsidized usefulness structurally unstable). It fails structurally unless demand-side integration — work selected and signed by paying customers, not chosen by miners — is designed in from the start, and no deployed system has this. The reversal conditions: a soundness break in cuPOW-class proofs would kill the weak form; sustained third-party paid demand routed through a live network, not arbitraged beside it as in the Together AI arrangement, would establish the strong form.

**Level 2, strategic.** Treat Pearl as the reference implementation to study, not the winner to back: first-mover chains in new PoW classes historically cede to second movers that fix the audit findings, and cuPOW's noise-injection soundness under adversarial workload selection is exactly the kind of finding that creates the opening (Section 3, amortization). Build positions in the verification toolchain — finite-field tensor proving in the TensorZKP line — which wins regardless of which chain wins. Watch hyperscaler mining behavior as the decisive centralization signal (F-D6), and watch for the first design that binds work to customer-signed demand at the protocol layer (F-D9) — that is the second-mover opening the critiques collectively specify. Ignore tolerance-based consensus schemes categorically (F-D4); treat floating-point puzzle designs as disqualified for any chain claiming hardware diversity, with the pinned-kernel monoculture path (F-D11) noted as technically real and strategically self-defeating.

**Level 3, immediately actionable.** Run the cross-vendor INT8 GEMM bit-exactness sweep on locally available hardware — the residual F-D4 verification; artifact: a test harness and a pass/fail matrix by vendor and generation, upgrading F-D4 from Modeled to Measured. Reproduce the cuPOW prover-overhead claim from the [Pearl monorepo](https://github.com/pearl-research-labs/pearl) against a plain cuBLAS GEMM baseline at matched sizes and precision; artifact: an overhead curve, the program's single highest-value measurement (F-D3, F-D7). Read the usefulness-gap study's methodology against the live network's current state — its data predates the Together AI endpoint's scaling, and the arbitrage critique of that arrangement (Section 4.1) needs checking against on-chain flows — and log the paid-demand fraction quarterly; artifact: a standing table in this document tracking the strong-form test of the Level 1 conclusion. Work through the Pass model's assumptions against Pearl's observed difficulty and price series to identify which trilemma leg the data says is giving way first; artifact: a short assumptions-versus-observations note appended to Section 4.2, and a regrade of F-D10 if the model's fit is poor.

## 7. Further Directions

Directions that survive these findings but exceed this document's scope, per the FableFrontiers.md Section 7 taxonomy.

*Science.* A soundness proof or break for noise-injected GEMM commitments under adversarial operand selection is the open theory problem the whole class now rests on. Progress-free puzzle constructions that consume *externally supplied* workloads without letting the supplier grind the puzzle would unify Topics C and D and currently exist nowhere. The hyperscaler-dominance threat model (F-D6) deserves formal treatment as a mechanism-design problem.

*Engineering.* A vendor-neutral deterministic tensor kernel specification — a consensus-grade subset of INT8 MMA behavior, analogous to what IEEE 754 did for floats — would de-risk every design in the class; no standards body currently owns this. Porting cuPOW-class proving to non-NVIDIA accelerators tests both the determinism claim and the decentralization story.

*Business.* The near-term monetizable surface is not mining but the verification toolchain and the audit market: every chain in this class needs the soundness analysis Pearl has not published, and the TensorZKP proving line has non-blockchain buyers in verifiable-compute markets. Fleet-liquidity products — routing idle inference capacity between spot rental and mining floor programmatically — are a small-team-sized opportunity with the discipline of F-D5 priced in.

*Social.* If inference-anchored PoW scales, mining demand and AI inference demand compete for the same silicon and the same power contracts, coupling cryptocurrency issuance to AI compute prices — an interaction regulators and grid planners have not modeled. The energy discourse also inverts: work that is AI-shaped but unpaid (F-D2) is harder to defend publicly than honest waste, because it invites the usefulness claim without earning it.
